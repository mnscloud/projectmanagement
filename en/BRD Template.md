## 2. Project Objectives
> *Guidance: Project objectives define the intended result of a project, which is frequently a material thing. Making objectives for your project is advantageous since it gives you and your team a clear direction for what they need to work toward. This can raise the likelihood of success for your team.*

- **Primary Objective:** [State the main, overarching goal of the project in 1-2 sentences.]
- **SMART Goals:**
  - **Specific:** [What exactly needs to be accomplished?]
  - **Measurable:** [How will success be measured? (e.g., specific KPIs, metrics, or targets)]
  - **Achievable:** [Is the goal realistic given current resources and constraints?]
  - **Relevant:** [How does this objective align with broader organizational goals?]
  - **Time-bound:** [What is the target completion date or milestone deadline?]

## 4. Business Requirements
> *Guidance: Business requirements gathering templates specify the properties of a proposed system from the perspective of the system's end user. These requirements are also referred to as stakeholder needs specifications. Delivering, satisfying, or meeting business requirements can be done using products, systems, software, and processes.*

- **Functional Requirements (What the system/process must do):**
  - **REQ-001:** [The system shall allow users to...]
  - **REQ-002:** [The process must include approval workflows for...]
- **Non-Functional Requirements (How the system/process must perform):**
  - **Performance:** [e.g., System must handle X concurrent users with <2s response time.]
  - **Security:** [e.g., Data must be encrypted at rest and in transit; role-based access control required.]
  - **Compliance:** [e.g., Must adhere to GDPR, HIPAA, or internal audit standards.]
- **User Stories / Stakeholder Needs:**
  - *As a* [type of user], *I want to* [perform an action], *so that* [I achieve a specific benefit].
